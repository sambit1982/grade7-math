🔢 Algebra

What is Algebra?

Algebra uses letters (called variables) to represent unknown numbers. Instead of saying "some number plus 3 equals 7", we write:

x + 3 = 7

Here, x is the variable — it stands for the number we need to find.

Expressions vs Equations

Expression

A math phrase without an equals sign.

2x + 5

Equation

A statement that two expressions are equal.

2x + 5 = 11

Solving One-Step Equations

To solve an equation, isolate the variable by doing the opposite operation on both sides.

Example: Solve x + 4 = 10

Subtract 4 from both sides:

x + 4 − 4 = 10 − 4
x = 6 ✓

Example: Solve 3x = 18

Divide both sides by 3:

3x ÷ 3 = 18 ÷ 3
x = 6 ✓

Solving Two-Step Equations

Sometimes you need two steps: first undo addition/subtraction, then undo multiplication/division.

Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 11

Step 1: Subtract 3 → 2x = 8

Step 2: Divide by 2 → x = 4 ✓

📏 Basic Trigonometry

Right Triangles & Ratios

Trigonometry is about the relationship between angles and sides in right triangles.

SOH CAH TOA

Remember this to never forget the three ratios:

SOH

sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse

CAH

cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

TOA

tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent

Example

Find sin θ, cos θ, and tan θ:

sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 6 / 10 = 0.6

cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 8 / 10 = 0.8

tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent = 6 / 8 = 0.75
